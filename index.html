About

My research sits at the intersection of robot learning, embodied AI, vision-language-action models, and real-world robotic autonomy. I work on systems that allow robots to interpret tasks, identify task-relevant entities, estimate environment states, reason over partial observations, plan actions, and execute long-horizon behaviors in complex environments. My broader goal is to build robotic systems that can continuously learn, adapt, and generalize across tasks, embodiments, and environments.

More broadly, I am interested in building general-purpose robotic intelligence through multimodal learning, structured reasoning, active perception, safe autonomy, and strong generalization across tasks and embodiments.

Selected Projects

Unified Learnable Robotic Cognition Framework

Research System

A modular Robotics Foundation Model (RFM) framework that integrates task interpretation, task-relevant state construction, active perception, state estimation, reasoning, planning, hierarchical decision-making, execution, and closed-loop monitoring. The framework is designed for long-horizon task execution in partially observable environments with strong emphasis on generalization and robustness.

Task-Oriented Active Object Detection

Perception / Embodied AI

A task-aware active perception pipeline that uses language-guided reasoning to identify relevant objects, detect them in the environment using Grounded SAM-based perception, and validate detections through multimodal reasoning for more reliable robotic execution.

Vision-Language State Estimation for Household Robotics

VLM-based State Understanding

A structured multi-prompt VLM framework for detecting objects, receptacles, held items, action outcomes, and environment state from first-person segmented observations, with contextual memory and physically grounded reasoning over task-relevant history.

Real-World Deployment on Boston Dynamics Spot

Robotic Deployment

Development and deployment of language-conditioned robotic behaviors on Boston Dynamics Spot, including active object search, navigation, articulated interaction, manipulation, and embodied decision-making for real-world robotic tasks.
