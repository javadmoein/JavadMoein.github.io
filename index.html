Javad Moein

I am a Robotics AI researcher at the Helping Hands Lab at Northeastern University, advised by Prof. Rob Platt. My research focuses on robotics learning, humaniod, manipulation and mobile manipulation, with an emphasis on imitation learning, reinforcement learning, geometric deep learning, and VLAs. I am particularly interested in improving generalization, sim-to-real, sample efficiency, and safe & robust learning.

My research goal is to enable robots and embodied intelligences to perform complex long-horizon tasks in real-world environments. To achieve this, I design hierarchical frameworks that combine high-level decision-making, planning, and reasoning with low-level control and action execution.

Research

  • Vision-Language-Action Models:Improve their generalization and sample efficiency by integrating geometric reasoning with semantic understanding of the environment.
  • Geometric Deep Learning:Incorporate geometric inductive biases into models, including geometry-aware representation learning and structured policy learning. SE(3)-equivariant and symmetry-aware architectures to leverage geometric structure for improved generalization and sample efficiency
  • Safe & Robust Learning:Detect potential failures early and develope recovery policies to prevent those failures before they occur.
  • Skill Learning: Learn a library of skills and skill dynamics model in latenet space for a skill-based and model-based RL to complete long horizan tasks with sparse reward.
  • Hirarchial Learning: high-level decision-making, planning, and reasoning with low-level control and action execution. .
  • Active Perception: Task-aware perception for identifying relevant objects, receptacles, spatial relations, and environment states.
